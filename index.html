Backstory

/

I'm based in San Francisco and Los Angeles. Before, I was on the product and growth team at Perplexity when the company had around 120 employees.

I'm Iranian-American. I like martial arts (BJJ, Muay Thai, Silat, third-degree black belt in TKD), and I enjoy reading philosophy (particularly Rumi, Aristotle, Tolstoy).

The best way to reach me is here.

Growing up, my older brother had a major impact on me. When I was four, he showed me his first-gen iPad and kept showing me things that changed how I saw the world.

Around seven, I was watching clips of Jackie Chan, Bruce Lee, and Chuck Norris on YouTube on that same device. For more than two years, I couldn't sleep at night because I wanted to be the closest thing to a modern ninja or samurai. I found a martial arts studio in my hometown in Maryland and never stopped.

At 14, my first job was instructing at that small martial arts school. I spent six hours a day preparing for my third-degree black belt test while coaching underbelt students and competing in tournaments. By 18, I had earned a black top gi and my third-degree black belt (I wore it around the house more than I should admit).

By the time I got to college, I had the same problem I always had. When something grabs me, I can't leave it alone. I just hadn't found the next thing yet. I told my brother I hadn't figured out what I wanted to do, let alone pick a major.

Around Christmas 2022, he sent me OpenAI's Playground. It was the iPad all over again. I couldn't put it down. For months I played with every AI tool I could find. Then one day he sent me another link, a startup called Perplexity. His text was short. "Might be worth sending a cold email."

That email eventually led me to join Perplexity's ~10-person product and growth team, where I worked for about a year while in school. I led the company's consumer verticals while focusing primarily on their education expansion efforts. That year or so at Perplexity made me realize startups were what I wanted to be in all along.

At the end of summer 2025, I packed my bags, left the East Coast, and moved west to get closer to Silicon Valley.
