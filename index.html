My Backstory

Last updated: March 2026

Growing up, my older brother who studied at Babson College had a major impact on me. When I was four, he showed me my first "high tech" toy — the first-gen iPad.

Around seven, I stumbled onto clips of Jackie Chan, Bruce Lee, and Chuck Norris on YouTube. For more than two years, I couldn't sleep at night because I wanted to be the closest thing to a modern ninja or samurai. I found the nearest martial arts studio in my hometown in Maryland and never stopped.

When I was 14, my first job was instructing at a small martial arts school. I spent six hours a day preparing for my third-degree black belt test, earned in May 2022, while coaching underbelt students and competing in national tournaments. By 18, I was awarded a black top gi (which I did wear around the house).

For months (to be specific, 17 months) I'd been telling my brother I hadn't figured out what I wanted to do as a career or let alone college major, but he noticed I kept tinkering with every AI tool I could get my hands on. One day he sent me a link to a startup called Perplexity with a short text: "might be worth sending a cold email."

That email led me to join Perplexity's ~10 person growth team, where I worked for about a year. I was leading the company's education expansion vertical, and realized startups were what I wanted all along.

So I packed my bags, left the East Coast, and moved west to get closer to Silicon Valley.

Coming soon on what's next.
