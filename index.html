My Backstory

Updated April 2026

/

I'm currently based in SF/LA. Before, I was an early hire on the growth and product team at Perplexity. I'm Iranian-American, I like martial arts (BJJ, Muay Thai, Silat, third degree black belt in TKD, etc), and the best way to reach me is on X.

Growing up, my older brother who studied at Babson College had a major impact on me. When I was four, he showed me my first "high tech" toy (the first-gen iPad).

Around seven, I stumbled onto clips of Jackie Chan, Bruce Lee, and Chuck Norris on YouTube. For more than two years, I couldn't sleep at night because I wanted to be the closest thing to a modern ninja or samurai. I found a martial arts studio in my hometown in Maryland and never stopped.

When I was 14, my first job was instructing at a small martial arts school. At that time, I spent six hours a day preparing for my third-degree black belt test while coaching underbelt students and competing in tournaments. By 18, I was awarded a black top gi (which I did wear around the house).

For months (to be specific, 17 months) I'd been telling my brother I hadn't figured out what I wanted to do as a career (or let alone pick a college major).

But I kept tinkering with every AI tool I could get my hands on. One day my brother sent me a link to a startup called Perplexity with a short text: "might be worth sending a cold email."

That email eventually led me to join Perplexity's ~10-person growth team, where I worked for about a year. I was leading the company's consumer verticals (mainly education expansion), and realized startups were what I wanted all along.

So I packed my bags, left the East Coast, and moved west to get closer to Silicon Valley.

Coming soon on what's next.
