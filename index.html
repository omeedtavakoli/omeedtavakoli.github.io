My Backstory

Last updated: January 2026

Growing up, my older brother who studied at Babson College made a huge impact on me. When I was four years old, he showed me my first "high tech" toy — the first-generation iPad.

From around seven, I stumbled across clips of Jackie Chan and Bruce Lee on a website called YouTube. For more than two years, I couldn't sleep at night because I wanted to be the closest thing to a "modern samurai". I googled the closest martial arts studio in my small hometown in Maryland, and never looked back.

My first job was as an instructor at a martial arts school. My mom always pushed me to serve people at the highest standard, and that job was my first attempt at figuring out what that actually meant.

I did 'meh' in high school. I did the bare minimum to look like I had everything under control. At that time, I was at the martial arts studio basically six hours every day, chasing my third degree black belt, coaching, and competing in tournaments. All of it felt more rewarding than school

Fast forward to mid 2024, I was in my second year of college in Boston. I spent the majority of my time trying to figure out what career path I wanted to pursue. Around that time, my brother sent me a link to a startup called Perplexity with a short text saying "might be worth sending a cold email." That cold email eventually led me to join Perplexity's ~ten person growth team, where I worked for about a year while in-school. During that time, I met some great people and realized that startups were the career path I wanted to pursue all along.

Soon after, I packed my bags, left the East Coast, and moved out West to get closer to Silicon Valley. More to come on what's next.
